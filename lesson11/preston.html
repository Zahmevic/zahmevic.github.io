Napoleon Dynamite Festival

The next 8th of July starts the Napoleon Dynamite Festival. Do you know why the name of this festival? This event is based in the movie “Napoleon Dynamite,” filmed in Preston by Jared Hess in 2003, and released in 2004. This film is about Napoleon, a high school student of Preston, he lives with his grandmother, his brother Kip, and his llama.
Napoleon meets Pedro, from Cd. Juárez, who is part of an exchange program, and Deb. They become good friends and one day Pedro decides to nominate himself to be the next class president, confronted with one of the most popular girls in the school.
Furthermore, Napoleon’s grandmother breaks her tailbone and needs to rest in the hospital, so she calls Napoleon and Kip’s uncle (Rico) to care for the two brothers. At that time, his uncle and Kip decide to associate to earn money with a project, but Rico starts to diffuse rumors about Napoleon, causing that Deb and Napoleon break their friendship.
During the Class President Election day, Pedro gives a speech, but Napoleon realizes that Pedro is in trouble, so he convinces the DJ to play a tape, jump to onto the stage and start to elaborate a dance number with the song. The happy ending is that Pedro wins the election, Napoleon’s grandmother return from the hospital, and Kip and his girlfriend married. In the Napoleon Dynamite Festival, people do fun activities that appear in the film, for example, the Tetherball Tournament, Tater Tot Eating Contest, or the Moon Boot Dance.
